Choosing the right horsepower (HP) ensures better cooling and energy efficiency

Room Size Recommended HP Ideal for
Up to 10 sqm 0.5–0.8 HP Small room
12 sqm 1.0 HP Small bedroom or office
13-16 sqm 1.5 HP Medium bedroom/living room
17–23 sqm 2.0 HP Large bedroom/living room
24–30 sqm 2.5 HP Large spaces/ offices

Other room factors to consider:

  • High ceilings, big windows, or direct sunlight → may require higher HP
  • Multiple occupants or heat-producing appliances → stronger cooling needed
  • Upper floors or poor insulation → increases cooling demand
  • Humid or coastal areas → choose units with dehumidifying and anti-corrosion features
Inverter Aircons
  • Cools the room faster by adjusting its compressor speed
  • Saves energy, quieter, and keeps the temperature consistent
  • Initial cost is higher, but saves more electricity cost over time
Non-Inverter Aircon
  • Turns the compressor on and off to maintain the temperature
  • Usually cheaper upfront, but uses more electricity over time
Electricity usage depends on:
  • Horsepower
  • Inverter Technology
  • Frequency of use
  • Energy Efficiency Rating (EER)

The higher the EER, the more efficient your unit is, as it cools faster while consuming less power. You can check your Meralco EER on the yellow label of the appliance itself.

Modern ACs include features that enhance convenience, efficiency, and indoor air quality (IAQ).

  • Wi-Fi / Smart Control: Allows you to control the unit remotely via a smartphone app and connect to voice assistants.
  • Sleep and Quiet Modes: Gradually raises the temperature while you sleep to save energy and maintain comfort. Quiet mode reduces noise while operating—ideal for bedrooms and quiet spaces.
  • Auto-Clean / Self-Diagnosis: Runs a drying cycle inside the unit to prevent mold or mildew growth, or displays an error code for quick troubleshooting.
  • Advanced Filters: Improve indoor air quality beyond basic dust filters. Common types include HEPA or high-density filters for fine particles, activated carbon filters for odors and gases, anti-bacterial filters to inhibit germs and mold, ionizers or plasma filters to neutralize airborne pollutants, and allergen or anti-dust mite filters for allergy-sensitive users.
  • Dehumidifying: Helps reduce moisture, removes dust, allergens, and odors, and prevents mold growth—especially useful in humid areas.

Consider upgrading if your unit is:

  • More than 10 years old
  • Consuming too much electricity
  • Needing frequent repairs
  • Not cooling the room effectively
  • Aircon filter cleaning: Every 2–3 weeks.

  • If you notice a musty smell from your aircon, it may be due to mold or bacteria buildup inside the unit. Regular cleaning or servicing can help solve this problem.
