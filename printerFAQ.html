Frequently Asked Question

Here are quick answers to questions shoppers ask the most.

  • Inkjet printers use liquid ink and are great for high-quality photos, images, and low-volume printing. These printers are ideal for home users who need vibrant and detailed prints.
  • Laser printers use toner powder and are ideal for fast, high-volume printing with sharp text. They’re best for fast, high-volume printing and produce sharp, professional-looking text documents, making them ideal for offices or frequent users.
  • Upfront cost: Inkjet printers are usually more affordable.
  • Long-term cost: Laser printers tend to have a lower cost per page, especially for heavy use.

If you print only occasionally, an inkjet might be enough. For regular or heavy use, a laser printer can save more over time.

If you want to print from your laptop, phone, or tablet without cables, yes! Most modern printers support Wi-Fi, Bluetooth, or mobile printing apps like Apple AirPrint or Google Cloud Print, allowing multiple devices to connect easily.

  • Ink cartridges: Used in inkjet printers and contain liquid ink, perfect for detailed color prints and photos.
  • Toner: Used in laser printers, it is a fine powder that lasts longer, making it ideal for large volumes of text documents.

If you print mostly documents, toner might be more cost-efficient. For creative projects or photo printing, ink cartridges are better.

An All-in-One (AIO) printer can print, scan, copy, and sometimes fax. If you want a single device for multiple tasks, it’s worth it. If you just need printing, a single-function printer might be cheaper.

  • Wireless connectivity (Wi-Fi, AirPrint, Google Cloud Print)
  • Duplex printing (automatic double-sided)
  • Print speed (pages per minute)
  • Print resolution (important for photos)
  • Cost of ink/toner replacements

For a small printing shop or business use, look for printers with:

  • High page yield and low cost per page (ink-tank or high-capacity cartridges)
  • Durable materials/media handling (supports heavier paper, or larger sizes)
  • Good speed and reliability

Recommended printers include all-in-ones with scanning/copying support.

Replace or refill your ink when printouts start looking faded, streaky, or patchy. If your printer’s ink level indicator shows low or empty, it’s time for a refill or replacement to avoid print quality issues.

Tip: Always check the page yield (how many pages one cartridge can print).
