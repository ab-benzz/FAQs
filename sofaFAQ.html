Frequently Asked Question

Here are quick answers to questions shoppers ask the most.

Measure your space before buying a sofa to make sure it fits well in your living area. Also, check the size of your doors, hallways, stairs, or elevator, especially if you live in a condo or apartment. Check out our Sofa Buyer’s Guide to learn more.

Sofas come in different types to match your comfort needs and room size:

  • Sofa: A standard three-seater ideal for families or shared living areas.
  • Loveseat: A cozy two-seater that fits perfectly in apartments or condos without taking up too much space.
  • Sectional: A modular sofa that can form L- or U-shapes, offering flexible seating for larger rooms.
  • Recliner: Designed for ultimate relaxation, recliners feature adjustable backrests and footrests—perfect for lounging, reading, or watching TV.
  • Sofa Bed: A multifunctional choice that easily converts into a bed, great for small homes or hosting overnight guests.
  • Armless Sofa: With its open and streamlined design, it helps make compact spaces look more spacious and modern.
  • Leather: A classic choice known for its durability and timeless appeal. It’s easy to clean and becomes softer and more beautiful over time—ideal for busy homes or those with pets.
  • Fabric: Offers a wide range of colors, textures, and patterns. It’s soft, cozy, and some types are treated to be stain-resistant—great for families or casual spaces.
  • Velvet: Luxurious and elegant, with a rich texture that adds depth to any room. Best for low-traffic areas or households without pets to maintain its plush look.
  • Synthetic Leather/Leatherette: Delivers the sleek look of real leather at a lower cost. It’s easy to wipe clean and resists spills, making it ideal for families or pet owners.

If you want a low-maintenance sofa, go for materials that resist stains and are easy to wipe down:

  • Leather: One of the easiest to clean—just wipe with a damp cloth to remove dust or spills.
  • Synthetic Leather/Leatherette: Similar to real leather but more affordable; it’s water-resistant and simple to maintain with mild soap and water. Synthetic leather is also a popular choice for pet owners due to how easy it is to clean up, as the leather will not absorb any liquids or leave stains when in contact with dirt.
  • Fabric with Stain-Resistant Treatment: Some fabric sofas are treated with special coatings that make cleaning up spills quick and easy.

Look for a sturdy frame made of kiln-dried hardwood for long-lasting durability. Common durable options include:

  • Solid Wood – Strong and reliable for everyday use.
  • Pinewood – Lightweight yet sturdy, ideal for various designs.
  • Beech – Dense and durable, perfect for high-quality sofas.
  • Vacuum regularly to prevent dust buildup and clean hard-to-reach spaces.
  • Rotate and fluff cushions to keep them even and prevent them from sagging.
  • Address spills and stains immediately, as untreated spills can soak deeper and cause discoloration.
  • Consider slipcovers to protect your sofa from accidental spills or pet damage.
  • Protect your sofa from direct sunlight to keep its synthetic leather or leatherette soft, vibrant, and long-lasting.
