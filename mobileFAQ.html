For average users
  • 128GB: A solid baseline for most people, offering enough room for everyday apps, games, and thousands of photos and videos.

For power users
  • 512GB or 1TB: Best for heavy gamers, content creators who record lots of high-resolution (such as 4K) videos, or anyone with large offline music or movie collections. These capacities provide ample space to keep everything stored on your device.

Display

  • Resolution: Refers to the number of pixels on the screen (e.g., FHD, QHD). Higher resolution means sharper text, clearer images, and more detailed videos.
  • Refresh Rate: Measured in Hertz (Hz), e.g., 60Hz, 90Hz, 120Hz. Higher refresh rates make animations, scrolling, and gaming appear smoother and more responsive.

Performance

Entry‑Level Processors: Best for web browsing, social apps, video streaming, messaging, and light apps.

  • Qualcomm Snapdragon 4 Series & 6 Gen (e.g., 6 Gen 4) – balanced budget performance
  • Mediatek Dimensity 600–9500 Series – solid basic 5G capability
  • Unisoc chips – found in budget phones

Midrange Processors: Best for multitasking, HD gaming, content consumption, and camera features.

  • Snapdragon 7 Series & 7s Gen 3 – smooth everyday and mid‑level gaming
  • Dimensity 800–8300 / 8200 Series – strong all‑around performance
  • Exynos 1380‑1580 (midrange Samsung) – balanced processing performance

Upper Midrange / High‑End Processors: Best for high‑refresh gaming, heavier multitasking, and advanced AI features.

  • Snapdragon 7+ / 8s Gen 3 – a performance boost over standard midrange
  • Dimensity 8300+ / 9200 / 8200 Plus – powerful Android chipsets

Flagship / Premium Processors (Top Tier Performance): Best for heavy gaming, pro‑grade photo/video editing, and advanced on‑device AI.

  • Snapdragon 8 Gen 3, Snapdragon 8 Elite, Snapdragon 8 Elite Gen 5 – top Android performance
  • MediaTek Dimensity 9300 / 9400 Series – flagship‑class power
  • Apple A18 / A19 Pro (iPhone) – industry‑leading performance and efficiency

Durability

Durable phones use high-quality materials like aluminum frames and Gorilla Glass screens. These materials help resist scratches, bending, and everyday wear and tear.

Water and Dust Resistance (IP Rating): Many smartphones come with an IP (Ingress Protection) rating that shows how resistant they are to water and dust.

  • IP67: Can handle splashes and brief submersion.
  • IP68: Offers better protection for deeper or longer water exposure.
