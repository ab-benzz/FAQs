Other room factors to consider:

  • High ceilings, big windows, or direct sunlight → may require higher HP
  • Multiple occupants or heat-producing appliances → stronger cooling needed
  • Upper floors or poor insulation → increases cooling demand
  • Humid or coastal areas → choose units with dehumidifying and anti-corrosion features
  • Mattress: A pad designed to support the person lying down. It provides comfort, distributes body weight evenly, and promotes proper spinal alignment during sleep.
  • Bed frame: Elevates and holds the mattress and/or foundation off the floor. The foundation (box spring, platform, or slatted base) sits directly under the mattress to provide solid, uniform support.

Make the most of every inch with smart bed choices:

  • Storage Beds: Built-in drawers help keep your room clutter-free.
  • Foldable or sofa beds: Give you extra sleeping space without taking up room during the day.
  • Double-deck or bunk beds: Maximize vertical space, perfect for shared rooms or compact spaces.
  • Pull-out or trundle beds: Offer a convenient extra sleeping option for guests without compromising your room’s layout.
