Frequently Asked Questions

Here are quick answers to questions shoppers ask the most.

Choosing the right laptop depends on your needs and how you’ll use it.

  • For work or school: Focus on performance, portability, and battery life.
  • For gaming or creative work: Look for a powerful processor, dedicated graphics card, and a high-resolution display.
  • Casual use: Simple tasks, such as browsing and streaming, are easily handled by standard entry-level laptops.
  • SSD (Solid State Drive): Uses flash memory, offering much faster read/write speeds and boot times.
  • HDD (Hard Disk Drive): Uses spinning magnetic disks to store digital data. It works by reading and writing data magnetically—best for storing files and light tasks.
  • 256GB: Enough for storing documents, music, and a few videos and pictures.
  • 512GB: Able to store higher-quality photos, store movies, and have enough space to install video games.
  • 1TB: Great for installing heavy programs for video or photo editing, storing larger games, and larger photos/videos.
  • 2TB: If you store lots of high-res videos, raw photos, or large datasets.

Some laptops allow you to upgrade storage and RAM, while others have fixed memory. You may ask our Abenson Personal Shoppers before purchasing if you plan to upgrade in the future.

For a smooth gaming experience, look for a laptop with:

  • RAM: At least 16GB for running modern games smoothly; 32GB preferred for AAA titles and streaming.
  • Processor: A high-performance CPU like Intel Core i7/i9 or AMD Ryzen 7/9.
  • GPU: A dedicated GPU such as NVIDIA GeForce RTX 4070 or AMD Radeon or higher for smooth gameplay at high settings.
  • Display: 144Hz (or higher) refresh rate for fluid motion; QHD or 4K resolution for immersive visuals.
  • Screen: IPS or OLED panel for vibrant colors and wide viewing angles.
  • Storage: Preferably an SSD (1TB or more) for faster game load times.
  • Cooling: Advanced thermal design to prevent overheating during long sessions.

You should consider upgrading your laptop when you notice:

  • Slow performance: Apps take longer to load, or multitasking feels sluggish.
  • Outdated software compatibility: Your laptop can’t run the latest apps or operating system updates.
  • Insufficient storage or memory: You constantly run out of space or RAM for your tasks.
  • Battery issues: Your laptop can’t hold a charge like it used to.
  • Hardware limitations for your needs: For example, gaming, video editing, or professional software require more powerful specs than your current laptop offers.

Refresh rate is the amount of times per second that an image is refreshed on your screen, which is measured using hertz (Hz). The higher the refresh rate or Hz, the smoother the motion of the image is on the screen. This makes movements look more natural and enhances the watching or gaming experience of the user.
